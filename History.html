Our History

Space exploration has a rich history that spans over six decades. Here's a broad overview of the major milestones in space missions:

1. Early Space Exploration (1940s-1960s)

1944-1945

German V-2 rocket becomes the first human-made object to reach the edge of space (100 km).

1957

Sputnik 1 - The Soviet Union launches the first artificial satellite into Earthâ€™s orbit, marking the beginning of the space age.

1961

Vostok 1 - Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin becomes the first human in space and the first to orbit Earth.

2. Apollo Program and the Moon Landings (1960s-1970s)

1969

Apollo 11 - NASA's mission lands Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin on the Moon, with Armstrong becoming the first human to set foot on the lunar surface.

1971

Apollo 15 - First use of the Lunar Roving Vehicle on the Moon.

4. The Space Shuttle Era (1980s-2000s)

1981

STS-1 - NASA launches the first Space Shuttle, Columbia.

1986

Challenger Disaster - Space Shuttle Challenger disintegrates 73 seconds into its flight, killing all seven astronauts on board.

1990

Hubble Space Telescope - Launched aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery, this telescope has provided some of the most detailed images of space ever captured.
